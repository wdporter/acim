Note Eastern tradition defines the Real as that which never changes. In Western philosophy, the Real is that which has necessary existence, as opposed to contingent existence. The Real exists by itself. If it can’t change, it can’t be threatened. The body is however contingent and subject to change, therefore by definition is not real.
